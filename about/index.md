      <h2>How I think</h2>
      <p>I am interested in the practical side of technology: the point where AI capability, data quality, system design, governance, and human judgement all meet.</p>
      <p>Most of the useful work happens between hype and refusal. That is where organisations have to choose what should be built, what should be automated, what has to remain legible, and who stays accountable when the system is wrong.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="about-card">
      <h2>Current areas of focus</h2>
      <ul>
        <li>Responsible AI adoption and the governance needed around it.</li>
        <li>AI strategy that connects ambition with operational capability.</li>
        <li>Data engineering foundations for better systems and better decisions.</li>
        <li>AI architecture, especially where technical choices meet operating models.</li>
        <li>Knowledge work, tooling, and the habits that make complex work easier to reason about.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="about-card about-card--wide">
      <h2>What you will find here</h2>
      <p>The writing archive holds essays, diaries, technical notes, and shorter reflections. Publications are used for longer-form public artefacts such as whitepapers, frameworks, reports, and guides.</p>
      <p>The emphasis throughout is practical clarity: what makes a system trustworthy, what makes a decision defensible, and what helps technical work survive the pressures of real delivery.</p>
