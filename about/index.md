    <h1>About</h1>
    <p class="article-hero__summary">I write about responsible AI adoption, AI strategy, data engineering, and AI architecture.</p>
  </header>

  <div class="article-prose">
    <p>I write about the practical side of technology: how AI, data, architecture, and human judgement come together when organisations try to do real work.</p>

    <p>I am interested in the space between hype and resistance. The useful work is usually clear enough to act on, honest enough to admit trade-offs, and grounded enough to survive contact with delivery.</p>

    <h2>What I focus on</h2>

    <ul>
      <li>Responsible AI adoption and the governance needed around it.</li>
      <li>AI strategy that connects ambition with practical capability.</li>
      <li>Data engineering foundations for better systems and better decisions.</li>
      <li>AI architecture, especially where technical choices meet operating models.</li>
      <li>Knowledge work, tooling, and the habits that make complex work easier to think about.</li>
    </ul>

    <h2>What you will find here</h2>

    <p>The blog remains the place for shorter posts, diaries, technical notes, and working reflections. Publications are reserved for more structured public artefacts such as whitepapers, frameworks, reports, and guides.</p>
  </div>
